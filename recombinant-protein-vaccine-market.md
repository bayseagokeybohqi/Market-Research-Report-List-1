<p><p>The recombinant protein vaccine market is categorized primarily into bacterial cell culture and mammalian cell culture types. Bacterial cell culture involves using bacteria, such as E. coli, to produce proteins that can stimulate an immune response. These vaccines are often simpler and cost-effective. In contrast, mammalian cell culture utilizes mammalian cells for protein production, allowing for post-translational modifications that enhance the efficacy and safety of the vaccines. Both methods play crucial roles in developing effective recombinant vaccines.</p></p>
