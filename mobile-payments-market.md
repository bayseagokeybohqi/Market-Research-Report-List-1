<p><p>The mobile payments market consists of two primary types: mobile wallets and mobile money. Mobile wallets, like Apple Pay and Google Pay, enable users to store bank cards and make transactions directly from their smartphones, leveraging Near Field Communication (NFC) technology. Conversely, the mobile money market focuses on services like M-Pesa, primarily in developing regions, where users can transfer funds, pay bills, and conduct transactions without traditional bank accounts, often using SMS for accessibility. Both enhance ease and security in financial transactions.</p></p>
